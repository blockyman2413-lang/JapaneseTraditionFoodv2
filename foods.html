Traditional Japanese Foods

Sushi

Started as preserved fish with rice. Benefits: fish protein and omega-3.

Ramen

Inspired by Chinese noodles and became popular in Japan after WWII. Benefits: filling and energy-rich.

Tempura

Introduced by Portuguese traders, then adapted by Japan. Benefits: seafood and vegetables.

Mochi

A sacred rice cake used in New Year traditions. Benefits: quick carbohydrate energy.

Soba

Buckwheat noodles eaten for long life, especially New Year. Benefits: fiber and minerals.

Takoyaki

Osaka street food made with octopus. Benefits: protein from seafood.
